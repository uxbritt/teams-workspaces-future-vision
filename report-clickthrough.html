Navigation Model

Flat vs. hierarchical — which scales for 50–500+ locations?

Key Finding

Hierarchical wins at scale

Flat dropdowns break at ~50 accounts. Enterprise platforms use folder trees, breadcrumbs, and persistent sidebars.

Competitor Insights

  • FranConnectTree nav with folders, scales to 1000+ units
  • ServiceTitanLocation tree with search + favorites
  • MailchimpFlat dropdown — breaks at 50 accounts
  • HubSpotPortal switching — context loss on switch

User Personas

3 Key Personas

FML market users with distinct needs at each hierarchy level

Super Admin

Enterprise Marketing Chief

  • Manage all locations & permissions
  • Share brand-approved assets
  • View rollup reports & approvals
  • Send org-wide communications

Regional Manager

Oversees Multiple Locations

  • Access & share brand assets
  • Manage regional locations
  • Report on regional performance
  • Identify struggling locations

Location Manager

Operations Focused

  • Access brand assets & templates
  • Start campaigns quickly
  • View workspace reporting
  • Manage contacts

All personas may need to manage their own marketing — campaigns, contacts, and automation at their level.
